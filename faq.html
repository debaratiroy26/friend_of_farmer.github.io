India is an agricultural country because two-thirds of its population is engaged in agricultural activities. Agriculture is a primary activity, which produces most of the food for people.

Rice is considered to be the principal food crop of West Bengal . Other major food crops include maize, pulses, oil seeds, wheat, barley, potatoes and vegetables. The state supplies nearly 66% of the jute requirements of India

The soil of West Bengal is broadly divided into four types - Mountain soils, Alluvial soils, Red soils, and Saline soils. Soils of West Bengal Mountain and Forest Soil. Old Alluvial pre. New Alluvial. Red Soil. Laterite Soil. Saline Soil.
